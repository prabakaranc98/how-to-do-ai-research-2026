# Research & Engineering Policy

**Last updated:** May 2, 2026  
**Author:** Pra Cha (prabakaranc98 / pracha.me)

This is the philosophy and operating policy for the public living lab described in `README.md`. The companion `northstar.md` translates this policy into company signals, problem spaces, skill stacks, and candidate research programs.

## 1. Philosophy
I pursue intelligence as an emergent technical *and* existential phenomenon.  
I do not chase surface-level applications or prompted demos.  
Instead, I attack **frontier problems** top-down: I select open, high-stakes hypotheses that frontier labs actually wrestle with, then execute rigorous, middle-to-advanced experiments that force depth in the methods that matter.

Learning > knowing.  
Fundamentals are reinforced *through execution* on real, scalable, substantive problems.

## 2. Operating Principles
- **Top-down only**: Start with a concrete frontier hypothesis. Never explore chronologically or for breadth’s sake.
- **Depth through implementation**: Every experiment must implement and ablate at least 2–3 advanced techniques at scale.
- **Continuous & emergent**: All work lives in one public “living lab” repository. Raw notebooks, failed runs, ablation logs, and surprising behaviors are committed in real time.
- **No surface customer apps**: Every line of code must advance true applied research or frontier engineering.
- **Evidence is the work itself**: The commit history, ablation logs, and reproducible experiments *are* my proof of extraordinary ability in research, engineering, and leadership.

## 3. Fluid Experimentation
I operate with strong conviction and open-minded execution.

Research direction comes from serious hypotheses, not random wandering. But the path is fluid: experiments evolve as evidence appears, failures expose better questions, and surprising results are treated as signal rather than noise.

The aim is continuous learning through public implementation: build, observe, ablate, revise, document, and publish.

## 4. Three-Layer Research Focus
All work deliberately spans and blends these layers — the exact problems frontier companies face daily.

**Layer 1 — Frontier AI**  
World models, long-horizon memory & continual learning, multimodal (video/audio) systems, agents & multi-agent coordination, alignment & safety, RL policies, interpretability, advanced architectures (Mamba, diffusion flows, hybrid state-space + attention, Port-Hamiltonian dynamics, etc.).

**Layer 2 — Scalable ML**  
Forecasting & prediction at scale, hyper-personalization & recommendation systems, bandits & online learning, algorithmic ML, scalable neural networks, statistical modeling & algorithms, multi-modal forecasting, large-scale training/inference optimization, prediction markets, social-media / recommendation algorithms.

**Layer 3 — Evidence Data Science**  
Causal ML & causal inference, probabilistic ML (ProbML), high-dimensional statistical modeling & inference, simulation & generative social networks, algorithmic statistics, rigorous validation & statistical power analysis, mechanistic interpretability, counterfactual reasoning.

Every experiment is designed to hit at least two layers simultaneously. The strongest ones hit all three.
